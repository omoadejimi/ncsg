## Who this guide is for

This guide is for:
- content designers
- UX writers
- product designers
- developers
- researchers
- product managers
- teams building government and fintech digital services for people in Nigeria.


## The principles

### 1. Clear content helps people complete tasks

Writing in plain language is not about reducing information. It is about making information easier to understand and use.
If a sentence makes people feel excluded or unsure, review the sentence rather than blaming the user.

### 2. Precise words build trust

In financial services, unclear messages about money can cause confusion and concern.
Use accurate and specific language so people understand what is happening and what they need to do.

### 3. Nigerian English is a recognised standard

Words such as airtime, BVN, POS and recharge are part of everyday digital language in Nigeria.
Use familiar terms consistently. Do not replace recognised Nigerian terms with unfamiliar alternatives.

### 4. Design for different user situations

In your design, consider people using:
- low-cost Android phones
- limited mobile data
- devices in bright sunlight
- low battery levels
- unreliable network connections.

### 5. Use evidence to make decisions

Base your recommendations on:
- user research
- support requests
- usability testing
- accessibility standards.

### 6. Improve the guide together

Anyone can [propose a change](/docs/operations/proposal-process); decisions made and their reasons are also public.
