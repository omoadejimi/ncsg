## Principles

Nigeria's diversity is the design constraint and the design opportunity. Inclusive writing means no user reads your product and concludes it was made for someone else.

### Names

Nigerian names come from hundreds of naming traditions. Never validate names against an English-centric pattern; allow long names, short names, hyphens, apostrophes and single names. Test your forms with **Oluwaseunfunmi**, **Ng'ang'a** and **Bem**.

### Gender

- Use "they" when gender is unknown.
- Ask for gender only when you genuinely need it, explain why, and include options beyond male/female where the regulatory context allows.

### Religion and culture

- Don't assume holidays. "Season's greetings" lands differently in Kano and in Enugu; prefer specific greetings used knowingly, or neutral ones.
- Avoid idioms tied to one culture when writing for all of Nigeria.

### Money and circumstance

Never write copy that shames low balances or small transactions. "Top up to continue" is an instruction; "Your balance is too low" plus a fee table is a judgement.

### Age and digital literacy

Avoid copy that assumes app fluency ("just toggle it in settings"). Spell out the path: "Go to Settings, then Notifications."
